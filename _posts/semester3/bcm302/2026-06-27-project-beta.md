## Project Beta: Bear Witness (v0.2.5)

For our Project Beta, we are presenting a playable Prologue of Bear Witness, our satirical visual novel on overconsumption (specifically blind box culture). 

What's included: A functional demonstration of Bear Witness's prologue chapter. Prototype character art is present. Features are to be polished ahead.

# Feedback Loop Documentation

## Scope Tighening

Informed by a majority consensus in the peer review, our primary iteration was to better refine our scope. Originally, we planned major route splits early in the story. However, after reviewing our timeline and the need for a stable prologue, we pivoted to a lighter "attribute system" instead, which allows the player to give inputs that have later-game influences in future, unreleased chapters.

![demonstration](/assets/images/bcm302_assets/source.jpeg)

Admittedly, this is a nuanced decision to make, as we acknowledge that the player craves immediate drama for dopamine releases. However, we also acknowledge the workload demand if we were to give dramatic branch-offs in the earliest portion of a narrative experience. 

To compensate, we inserted varying dialogue after players shape the bear's identity (curiosity, social, impulsiveness). These choices allow the player to be cognitively reaffirmed that their input is digested by other characters before an eventual branch-off arrives.

![demonstration](/assets/images/bcm302_assets/consequence.jpeg)

## Refining "Gacha Scene" 

Initially, based on evidences from Peer Review 1, about three peers expressed concern that the gacha scene felt too much like a reward, which is an unwanted consequence of our satirical intent. In response, we rewrote the scene to feel uncomfortable and critical. 

After the change, our testers find the in-game exchange stressful rather than "simply fun", which aligns with our goal of critiquing impulsive spending rather than glorifying it. However, concerns regarding the game's pacing is present, and has been noted. This issue will be addressed in future iteration, allowing the player to better engage with the plot psychologically.
