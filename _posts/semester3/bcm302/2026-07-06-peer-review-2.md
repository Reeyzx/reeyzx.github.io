# [Project 1 -- Mindful Learning](https://wwh1498.wixsite.com/bcm-302/post/project-beta-reduce-kids-study-stress)

Mindful Learning demonstrates a strong developmental trajectory by pivoting from a YouTube channel to a dedicated Wix blog, alongside shifting the primary audience from students to parents, effectively addressing the platform limitations and user-base mismatches identified in Peer Review I.

As a constructive nudge for the final iteration, while the strategic pivot to position parents as the primary audience is a strong developmental step, officially stating this intent inside the audience-facing side of the website would be a beneficial move. Currently, the Wix site lacks a formal disclaimer (usually a dedicated webpage) to restrict institutional use. It is especially important as the education curriculum in Hong Kong currently lacks a standardized "stress relief" course, which may fuel institutions to impose this platform on their students as a lazy solution in response to this hollow. By clarifying that this is strictly a parent-led, at-home tool, the project's original vision will be safeguarded.

Regarding production methodology, the use of AI-generated video introduces some unintended visual "artefacts" (e.g., a character swallowing a whole nut). While it is crucial to be aware of child safety (a clueless kid might stumble upon the video, swallow a nut, and choke), these glitches add a layer of surreal humor that may significantly boosts entertainment value. In today's popular media, engagement often stems from unexpectedness. I suggest leaning into these artefacts intentionally for the project's final release, as a fine touch of uniqueness might be the key to capturing and retaining audience attention in a saturated market, in the long run.

# [Project 2 -- QueueCue](https://fizaraees1530.wixsite.com/queuecue)

QueueCue addresses a highly relevant utility addressing the need for a real-time queue tracker. The developmental trajectory shows effort refining the UI based on user feedback, specifically regarding the strengthening of the feature of nearby comparison.

It is noted that the project remains confined to UI interactivity rather than data functionality. While the FIST principle is successfully applied to keep the UI design straightforward, a functional test of the core utility is highly anticipated (how it feels to use the app). Currently, the wait times are static placeholders. As noted in the Beta, plans such as optimizing data accuracy or expanding locations could be technically premature before the underlying data pipeline (how wait-times information actually enter the system) hasn't been developed yet.

Furthermore, it would be beneficial to expand audience reach in the feedback loop. The iterative survey revealed 100% intent to use the app, but this plausibly reflects acquiescence bias (agreement bias), where respondents reacts positively after being shown a well-curated pitch, rather than by hands-on testing the actual product. 

For the final iteration, I recommend shifting from visual FIST to functional FIST. The project's credibility would increase sharply once the product is being deployed in practical use. Clearing this methodological hurdle will significantly enhance the project's credibility before feature expansion.
