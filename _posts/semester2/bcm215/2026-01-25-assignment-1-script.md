# Content

Indie developers succeed by reframing genres within their constraints. Shaw calls this working with limitations rather than against them. In the context of Rocket League. By adding rocket-powered cars to football, they attracted players who'd never touch FIFA, which is a dedicated football simulator. This is called genre mainstreaming.

Last semester, I built a raycast vehicle in Roblox Studio. To explore how zero-budget developers can learn from the former example's success, I will utilize my existing product to construct a tank game concept, transforming military simulators into an arcade experience."

In this project, I adapt a mix of two framework: Cultural studies analyzes how design decisions function as accessibility interventions that broaden or narrow appeal. Political economy examines how limitations shape feasibility. The central question is: How much quirks achieves ideal results? When does it become counter-intuitive?

Studying from different aspects, each session focus on one experiment. After building each prototype, I play-test and see how the design affects cognitive load, genre identity, and tactical depth. I will then document what works and what’s considered trade-offs."

My framework draws on three peer-reviewed sources. As mentioned, Shaw's 2010 work on indie game production culture grounds my political economy analysis, examining how zero-budget constraints shape creative choices. Scharkow and colleagues' 2015 research on genre preferences and player motivations provides my framework for understanding how design decisions shape audience appeal across casual and hardcore player types. Rapp's 2018 research on autoethnography validates my designer-player-researcher methodology, demonstrating how self-reflection generates legitimate analytical insights without external playtesting."

This project is completed in two halves. The first half contains one to two laboratory experiments. These initial sessions will be documented and analyzed for the Assessment 2 progress report due March 1st, serving as the midway keystone. The second half completes the remaining experiments, wrapping up all findings for Assessment 3. The final contextual report and Digital Artefact, submitted March 29th, synthesize insights from both halves.
