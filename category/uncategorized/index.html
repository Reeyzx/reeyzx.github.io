    [Tragically, the About Me section is yet working in progress. Nevertheless, below is a short text regarding my background & future]

    Hello, this is Lau Kai Lok, but I also go by Michael. As the domain of this blog suggests, on most online platforms, I’m known as Ree(_something something here).

    In the past several years, I worked as a hobbyist game developer on Roblox, specializing in environment & lighting design. Recently, I’ve also picked up 3D modelling on Blender, and worked on experimental programming projects via the assistance of AI technology. I aim to continuously broaden my understanding of game development as a whole, and to produce serious, well-thought content in the coming future.

    Three sites that I follow

    There is a charm working in solitude. However, it’s always beneficial to stay engaged with the community to keep up to date with the latest news, and what everyone else are pursuing. Below are three of all the different sites that I’ve frequented, that I believe is worth sharing.

    • Mail

    Roblox Developer Forum

    https://devforum.roblox.com

    Boom, the big guy in the room — This is what I would refer to as “the big ocean”. Intended as a source of official news regarding the Roblox Platform, the Forum is also a centralized location for everyone to engage in talks of various topics, ranging from technical support to bug report.

    Let There Be (Unified) Light! Unified Lighting is Fully Live. Roblox Rendering Team. (2025, July 23). [Video]. Roblox Developer Forum.

    What I like about this platform

    Simple, clear, concise. This is the equivalent of an encyclopedia on Roblox. Since my use of this platform is for checking the latest news, it gets the information in a straightforward way.

    • Reddit

    [Reddit] r/robloxgamedev

    https://www.reddit.com/r/robloxgamedev

    It goes without saying that there’s always a subreddit in every single topic & niche. As the name suggests, this is a site created for Roblox game developers.

    • YouTube

    [Youtube] Dabidar

    https://www.youtube.com/@DabidarZ

    A Roblox Youtuber who specializes in experimental programming. Unlike other mainstream Roblox Youtube channels, this channel heavily lean towards invention showcasing, instead of providing educational tutorials.

    Case Study: Identifying a media niche

    Among these media sites & platforms above, I find a video by Dabidar particularly intriguing. Here’s the full video:

    I happened to stumble upon this video while playing the role as a developer who has shallow knowledge on vehicle chassis programming, but wishes to deepen the understanding of how ray cast car physics work in Roblox (and other game engines).

    While watching this video, my initial reaction was simple fascination over the suspension work (the fact that the car doesn’t visibly glitch or bug out when bumping into rough terrain).

    Green dots scattered at the top/bottom of each wheels, likely ray cast origins/intersection points

    Nevertheless, due to the fact this this was merely a demo video, there’s a lack of instructions. Thus, I was unable to completely grasp the exact programming approach to this car system. Still, there were minor takeaways from this video demonstration. Here’s an excerpt from my documented notes:

    - no physical wheel in ray cast chassis
- ray fires (casts) into the ground, ray & ground intersection point represent the coordinate of the bottom of a hypothetical wheel
- (?) wheels spin based on distance, unrelated to physics
...

    Perhaps due to my role as a developer with existing (albeit shallow) programming knowledge, I would call myself one of the target audience. But instead of direct information-feeding, I would label this video as some sort of an inspiration.

    Interesting Discovery

    Interestingly, some, but not everyone reacted the same way. From what I see in the comment section, I notice roughly 3 distinctive crowds of people.

    Group 1: General Praises

    “bro is cooking hard”

    “i love to see the suspension working!!! its amazing!!”

    “i just love how it goes over the bump sooo cool i can watch this for hours ngl lol btw how did u get the parts to regen and move?”

    Group 2: Confusion & Questioning

    “How to make this”

    “ah yes, something i cannot comprehend.”

    “How did you make the car model work?”

    Group 3: Sarcasm & Downplay

    “Bro made a part that spam tweens parts on repeat just to show case this lol”

    “not first one if in build a boat, heck they make things except boats, mechanics, car simulation, you guess it.”

    “Great work but it’s definitely not first ever, I made the entire ___ game tanks physics with custom raycast physics :)”

    Analyzing Behaviors

    Without naming individuals, there appears to be a pattern where individuals who delivers comments in Group 1 are noticeably shorter, and more simple. We can identify keywords, such as “good”, “cool”, “cooking”, most of which has an aim of congratulating the Youtuber for making something impressive. A few also compares this work to other existing games on Roblox, citing this version of car physics is better than that of other games. Implying a likelihood that Group 1 mostly falls under everyday players who seeks fun & entertainment.

    On the other hand, comments from Group 2 are evidently more frustrated. Most of which write down comments to request tutorial videos, likely due to an unmet expectation of seeing an easy answer to building a ray cast car system in this video. Some individuals also voice feelings that they are unable to achieve what the Youtuber did, which might be signs of self depreciation and a loss of confidence. From this cluster, there is a pattern that people under this group approach this video with an intent to learn (similar to my personal situation), but ended up not receiving direct answers, nor getting any takeaways that would aid their work. We can conclude that this group are fellow developers on Roblox, who possesses a more limited technical understanding than myself.

    Lastly, we have a more diverse group of people who forms up Group 3, the criticizing camp. taking the comments above, we can see who appears to be a casual critic, a player who display sufficient knowledge in a specific popular Roblox game, as well as a veteran developer who has already coded a battle tank using the same mechanics. Though they came from different backgrounds, all of which are engaging in a comparison behavior, putting the Youtuber’s ray cast car, and their own knowledge on a balance scale.

    Without diving deep into the sophisticated topic of psychology, I’ll leave my guesses on why they would do that. Based on my experience in Roblox, the players’ communities are close knitted, and there is a heavy sense of belonging & loyalty, where one would vouch for their favorite games and developers. Developers on the other hand, often engage competitively, due to high competitions on the platform.

    Perhaps this environment would foster a need for some individuals to establish boundaries, with an intent to define who’s more prestigious, and whose work worth more..

    Conclusion

    Based on the commentary of Dabidar’s Roblox ray cast car chassis demonstration video, we ended up with discoveries that appears to probe underneath the technical showcase, which reveals the demographics composition of this media niche, and possibly, Roblox community as a whole.

    • Entertainment driven casual players
    • Beginner level game developers
    • “Gatekeepers” of different backgrounds

  • About Media Niche

    Introduction

    Many people dreams about being a content creator nowadays — Youtuber, “Influencer”, Instagram Models, and all that jazz.
    In today’s creative industry, it is undeniable that anyone can get an easy start on their grand endeavor, but is an easy start a good start? Obviously not the case.

    For privacy reasons, I may not be able to illustrate with examples in this blog, but feel free to try a fun experiment on the spot.
    Grab your phone, pick a media platform, and search up any obscure content creators.

    Ask yourself — What do they have in common?

    Today’s a cooking tutorial.
    Tomorrow’s a trip to a theme park.

    A critical lack of orientation.

    Media Niche: An Overlooked Element

    Fish in a comfy tank. Well fed, just enough space to assert dominance.

    Fish in the ocean. Freedom of movement. Well, there’s always a bigger fish.

    Think of a body of water as all kinds of topic available. Nobody is stopping you to choose a living in the ocean. However, competition is fierce, and there’s a lot many fishes in the ocean.

    How do you even shine, to stand out from the crowd? Perhaps, it may be more convenient to know your niche. Pick the right battle, and get the right reward.

    So, what exactly is a media niche?

    A media niche is a highly specific topic, interest, or group of people that a particular media product, service, or platform targets, catering to their unique needs, preferences, and characteristics that distinguish them from a broader audience. 

    Google Gemini

    Let’s get our vision clear — We need something specific, perhaps oddly particular, and with high uniqueness.

    So, a media niche isn’t going to be about an everyday Joe discussing his grand ambition of being a renowned traveler, or a respected teacher. Something more on point, just like a needle poking a spot.
